How the tournament works

1 · The draw

9 players were drawn at random into three groups of three:

  • Group A: Anderson Yamamoto, Shahed Abu Qamar, Ashwin Risbood
  • Group B: Adham Badr, Francesco Fiori, Kartikeya Gokhale
  • Group C: Sarat Chebrolu, Pratik Moona, Dinesh

2 · Group stage

  • Each group is a round-robin — every player plays the other two (3 matches per group).
  • Every match is best of 3 games. First to win 2 games wins the match.
  • Each game is played to 11 points (win by 2 at deuce, e.g. 12–10) — there are no draws.
  • Winning a match = 1 point.

3 · Ranking

Players in a group are ranked by:

  1. Points (matches won), then
  2. PF — most total points scored across all their games, then
  3. PA — fewest total points conceded.

The player at the top of each group qualifies. The three group winners are seeded 1–3 by the same rule (points, then PF).

4 · Playoffs

The 3 seeds play three best-of-3 matches:

  • Q1 — Seed 1 v Seed 2. Winner goes straight to Q3; the loser drops to Q2 for a second chance.
  • Q2 — Loser of Q1 v Seed 3. Winner advances to Q3; the loser is eliminated.
  • Q3 (Final) — Winner of Q1 v Winner of Q2. The winner is the champion.

The top two seeds get a second chance — a single loss won't knock them out. Seed 3 must win twice to take the title.
